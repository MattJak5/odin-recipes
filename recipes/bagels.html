Description

An easy homemade New York-Style bagel recipe

total time is around 2 hours from start to finish

Ingredients

  • 6g active dry yeast
  • 10g sugar
  • 300ml warm water
  • 440g bread flour
  • 6g salt

Instructions

  1. Mix 120ml of water in with the sugar and yeast. let them sit for 5 minutes before stiring to dissolve the ingredients.
  2. Mix the flour and salt in a large bowl. Make a well in the middle and pour in the yeast mixture from step 1.
  3. Pour 80ml of the warm water into the well. Start mixing together adding the remaining 100ml of water as needed, until the dough is moist and firm
  4. Knead the dough on a floured work surface for 10 minutes until the dough is smooth and elastic. At this point the dough should be firm and stiff.
  5. Brush a large bowl with a thin layer of oil and turn the dough to coat. Cover the bowl with damp towel. Let rise for 1 hour in a warm place (or until it has doubled in size). Punch the dough down and leave for another 10 minutes.
  6. Divide the dough into 8 pieces (roughly 95g each) and shape each peice following the video bellow

    7. Bagel shaping technique

  7. Once shaped, place the bagels on a baking sheet covered with greaseprrof paper. Cover with a damp towel and leave to rest for 10 minutes.
  8. Meanwhile, preheat the oven to 220ÂºC and bring a large pot of water to the boil.
  9. Once the water has boiled, reduce the heat and use a slotted spoon to add the bagels to the water. Boil for 1 - 2 minutes then flip over and boil for another 1 - 2 minutes.
    • The longer the boil, the chewier the bagel!
    • Adding sugar/maple syrup/bicarbonate of soda into the water before boiling will give the crust a nice brown colour, and in the case of bicarb a subtle pretzel-like flavour
  10. Once boiled you can add additional toppings like sesame seeds or poppy seeds. Then place them on a greaseprrof paper lined baking sheet
  11. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until golden brown.
  12. Cool on a wire rack before cutting in and topping
